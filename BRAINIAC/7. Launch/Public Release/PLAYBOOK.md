# PLAYBOOK: Public Release

## Objective

Craft and distribute professional release notes that maximize reach, generate press coverage, and convert attention into users.

## Launch Priority

**Product Hunt is the #1 launch platform.** Execute the full [Product Hunt Playbook](../Product%20Hunt/PLAYBOOK.md) and [Checklist](../Product%20Hunt/CHECKLIST.md) before anything else. After Product Hunt, submit to every platform listed in the [Directories & Launch Platforms](../Directories%20%26%20Launch%20Platforms.md) guide.

## Release Notes Structure

Every public release should follow this format:

### 1. Headline
- One sentence. State what the product does and for whom.
- No jargon. No buzzwords. Clear value proposition.

### 2. The Problem
- 2-3 sentences describing the pain your target user faces today.
- Use specific, relatable language. Reference real frustrations.

### 3. The Solution
- What your product does differently.
- Focus on outcomes, not features. "Save 10 hours/week" beats "AI-powered automation."

### 4. Key Features (3-5 max)
- **Feature Name** — One-line benefit statement.
- Lead with the feature that solves the biggest pain.
- Skip anything that doesn't directly drive value for the user.

### 5. Social Proof
- Beta user quotes, waitlist numbers, or early metrics.
- If you have none yet, state the problem validation data from Phase 0 (Idea).

### 6. Call to Action
- One single CTA. Don't split attention.
- Examples: "Try it free", "Join the waitlist", "Get early access."

### 7. Founder Note (Optional but Recommended)
- 2-3 sentences. Why you built this. What drives you.
- Product Hunt data shows 70% of #1 products include a personal founder story.

## Distribution Channels

After crafting the release notes, distribute across these channels in order:

1. **Product Hunt** — Primary launch platform. See [Product Hunt Playbook](../Product%20Hunt/PLAYBOOK.md).
2. **Directories & Platforms** — Submit to all platforms in [Directories & Launch Platforms](../Directories%20%26%20Launch%20Platforms.md).
3. **X/Twitter** — Thread format. Build in Public style.
4. **LinkedIn** — Long-form post targeting your ICP's professional network.
5. **Reddit** — Relevant subreddits only. Provide value, don't spam.
6. **Indie Hackers** — Post in "Launch" category with transparency on metrics.
7. **Email List** — Dedicated launch email to your waitlist/subscribers.
8. **Community Groups** — Slack, Discord, WhatsApp groups where your audience lives.
